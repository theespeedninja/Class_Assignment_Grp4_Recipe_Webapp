    Pilau

    Fragrant spiced rice slow-cooked with tender beef, caramelised onions, and a rich blend of pilau masala — a celebrated Kenyan celebration dish traditionally served at weddings and festive gatherings.

    • ⏱ Prep: 20 min
    • Cook: 45 min
    • Serves: 6
    • Difficulty: Medium

    Ingredients

    • 500 g basmati or long-grain rice
    • 400 g beef (cubed)
    • 2 tbsp pilau masala spice mix
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
    • 3 tbsp cooking oil
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 litre water or beef stock

    Directions

    1. Heat oil in a heavy pot over medium heat. Fry onions until deeply golden and caramelised — about 15 minutes.
    2. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 2 minutes until fragrant.
    3. Add beef and pilau masala. Brown the meat on all sides, about 8 minutes.
    4. Wash the rice until the water runs clear, then add to the pot.
    5. Pour in the water or stock. Season with salt. Bring to the boil.
    6. Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and simmer for 25–30 minutes until the rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.
    7. Let it rest, covered, for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with kachumbari.

    Chef's Tips

    • The key to Pilau is deeply caramelised onions — don't rush this step.
    • Use whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, cumin) to make your own pilau masala for extra depth.
    • Soaking the rice for 30 minutes before cooking gives fluffier results.

    Nyama Choma

    Kenya's iconic grilled meat — smoky, tender, and best enjoyed outdoors with cold drinks, friends, and kachumbari.

    • ⏱ Prep: 15 min
    • Cook: 60 min
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 1 kg goat or beef (bone-in cuts work best)
    • 1 tsp coarse salt
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • Kachumbari (fresh tomato-onion salsa) to serve

    Directions

    1. Cut meat into large pieces. Rub with salt and drizzle lemon juice over all sides.
    2. Light charcoal and allow to reach a steady glowing heat (no open flames).
    3. Place meat on the grill. Cook slowly over medium heat, turning every 10–15 minutes.
    4. Grill for 45–60 minutes until the meat is cooked through with a smoky, slightly charred exterior.
    5. Rest the meat for 5 minutes. Chop into smaller pieces and serve immediately with kachumbari and ugali.

    Chef's Tips

    • Patience is everything — low, slow charcoal heat is key to authentic Nyama Choma.
    • Do not over-marinate; purists use only salt and lemon to let the meat speak for itself.

    Ugali & Sukuma Wiki

    Kenya's everyday staple — firm maize porridge paired with nutritious sautéed kale.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: 30 min
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups maize flour (unga wa mahindi)
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 bunch kale (sukuma wiki), finely sliced
    • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 tbsp cooking oil
    • Salt to taste

    Directions

    1. Sukuma Wiki: Heat oil in a pan. Fry onions until soft. Add tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes.
    2. Add sliced kale, stir well, cover and cook for 8–10 minutes on medium heat. Season with salt.
    3. Ugali: Bring 4 cups of salted water to the boil in a heavy pot.
    4. Reduce heat to medium. Gradually pour in the maize flour while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
    5. Keep stirring vigorously with a wooden spoon for 10–12 minutes until the ugali is stiff, smooth, and pulls away from the sides of the pot.
    6. Serve ugali moulded onto plates alongside the sukuma wiki.

    Githeri

    A nourishing mix of boiled maize and beans sautéed with tomatoes and onions.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: 50 min
    • Serves: 5
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups dried maize (soaked overnight)
    • 1 cup dried kidney beans (soaked overnight)
    • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 tbsp cooking oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    Directions

    1. Boil the soaked maize and beans together in salted water for 40–45 minutes until tender.
    2. In a separate pan, fry onions in oil until golden. Add tomatoes and cook down to a sauce.
    3. Add the drained boiled maize and beans to the sauce. Stir together and simmer for 5 minutes.
    4. Season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

    Mukimo

    A colourful Kikuyu mash of potatoes, peas, maize and greens — hearty and comforting.

    • ⏱ Prep: 15 min
    • Cook: 40 min
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Medium

    Ingredients

    • 500 g potatoes, peeled and quartered
    • 1 cup green peas
    • 1 cup boiled maize kernels
    • 1 cup pumpkin leaves or spinach, chopped
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tbsp butter or oil (optional)

    Directions

    1. Boil potatoes, peas, and maize in salted water until the potatoes are completely soft.
    2. Add the chopped greens and cook for a further 3–4 minutes.
    3. Drain most of the water, leaving a little for mashing.
    4. Mash everything together until well combined. Add butter if desired. Season with salt.
    5. Serve as a main dish or side alongside stewed meat.

    Biryani

    A grand layered dish of marinated chicken and fragrant basmati rice — the jewel of the Swahili coast kitchen.

    • ⏱ Prep: 30 min
    • Cook: 60 min
    • Serves: 6
    • Difficulty: Hard

    Ingredients

    • 600 g chicken pieces
    • 3 cups basmati rice
    • 200 ml plain yogurt
    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 4 cloves garlic + 1 tbsp ginger (paste)
    • 2 tsp biryani spice mix (or garam masala)
    • 1 tsp turmeric
    • Saffron or food colouring dissolved in 3 tbsp warm milk
    • 4 tbsp ghee or oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander and fried onions to garnish

    Directions

    1. Marinate chicken in yogurt, garlic-ginger paste, biryani spice, turmeric, and salt for at least 1 hour (overnight is best).
    2. Fry sliced onions in ghee until deeply golden; set half aside for garnish.
    3. Cook marinated chicken in the remaining fried onions over medium heat until tender, about 20 minutes.
    4. Par-cook basmati rice in salted boiling water until 70% done. Drain well.
    5. In a large heavy pot, layer half the rice, then all the chicken, then the remaining rice.
    6. Drizzle the saffron milk and remaining ghee over the top layer.
    7. Cover tightly with foil then the lid. Cook on the lowest heat for 25–30 minutes (dum cooking).
    8. Gently fold through before serving. Garnish with fried onions and fresh coriander.

    Mandazi

    Soft, lightly sweet East African doughnuts made with coconut milk — a beloved breakfast and street-food snack.

    • ⏱ Prep: 15 min
    • Cook: 20 min
    • Serves: 12
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 3 cups all-purpose flour
    • 200 ml coconut milk
    • 3 tbsp sugar
    • 1 tsp instant yeast
    • 1 tsp cardamom powder
    • Pinch of salt
    • Oil for deep frying

    Directions

    1. Mix flour, sugar, yeast, cardamom, and salt in a bowl. Gradually add coconut milk to form a soft dough.
    2. Knead for 5–8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    3. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 45 minutes until doubled.
    4. Roll out on a floured surface to about 1 cm thick. Cut into triangles or squares.
    5. Heat oil to 170°C. Fry mandazi in batches for 2–3 minutes each side until golden brown.
    6. Drain on paper towels. Serve warm with chai (tea).

    Chapati

    Flaky, layered Kenyan flatbread — hand-rolled and pan-fried to golden perfection.

    • ⏱ Prep: 20 min
    • Cook: 30 min
    • Serves: 8
    • Difficulty: Medium

    Ingredients

    • 3 cups all-purpose flour
    • 3 tbsp vegetable oil (plus extra for frying)
    • 1 cup warm water
    • 1 tsp salt

    Directions

    1. Combine flour and salt. Rub in 2 tbsp oil. Gradually add warm water, kneading to form a soft, smooth dough.
    2. Rest the dough covered for 20 minutes.
    3. Divide into 8 equal balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle.
    4. Brush with a little oil, fold in half, brush again, fold in quarters. Roll out again to a round.
    5. Cook on a hot dry pan (or with a little oil) for 2–3 minutes per side until golden spots appear.
    6. Keep warm wrapped in a clean cloth until serving.

    Samosa

    Crispy triangular pastries packed with spiced minced beef and peas — a beloved Kenyan party snack.

    • ⏱ Prep: 30 min
    • Cook: 25 min
    • Serves: 20
    • Difficulty: Hard

    Ingredients

    • 20 samosa pastry wrappers (or spring roll pastry)
    • 300 g minced beef
    • ½ cup green peas
    • 1 onion, finely diced
    • 2 tsp cumin + 1 tsp coriander powder
    • ½ tsp chilli powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil for deep frying
    • Flour paste (2 tbsp flour + water) for sealing

    Directions

    1. Fry onions until soft. Add mince and cook until browned. Add spices, peas, and salt. Cook 5 more minutes. Cool completely.
    2. Lay a pastry wrapper flat. Place a spoonful of filling near one end.
    3. Fold into a triangle, tucking the pastry tightly around the filling. Seal the edge with flour paste.
    4. Deep fry in oil at 170°C for 3–4 minutes until golden and crispy.
    5. Drain on paper towels. Serve with tamarind or chilli sauce.

    Matoke

    Green bananas simmered in a rich beef and tomato stew — a comforting East African classic.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: 40 min
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 6 green bananas (matoke), peeled and halved
    • 300 g beef, cubed
    • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Water or stock as needed

    Directions

    1. Fry onions in oil until golden. Add beef and brown on all sides.
    2. Add tomatoes and cook until they break down into a sauce, about 8 minutes.
    3. Add the peeled green bananas and enough water or stock to just cover.
    4. Cover and simmer for 25–30 minutes until bananas are tender and the stew has thickened.
    5. Season and serve hot.

    Kachumbari

    A bright, zesty fresh salsa of tomatoes, onions, coriander, and lemon — no cooking required.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: No cooking
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 3 medium ripe tomatoes, finely diced
    • 1 red onion, finely diced
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • Small bunch fresh coriander, chopped
    • ½ fresh chilli, finely chopped (optional)
    • Salt to taste

    Directions

    1. Finely dice tomatoes and onion. Place in a mixing bowl.
    2. Add chopped coriander and chilli if using.
    3. Squeeze over the lemon juice, season with salt, and toss everything together.
    4. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately or chill for 15 minutes.
    Coastal Kenyan

    Fish Fry

    Fresh fish marinated in garlic, lemon, and spices — then deep-fried to crispy golden perfection.

    • ⏱ Prep: 15 min
    • Cook: 25 min
    • Serves: 3
    • Difficulty: Medium

    Ingredients

    • 3 whole fish (tilapia or snapper), cleaned and scored
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 1 tsp cumin + 1 tsp coriander powder
    • ½ tsp turmeric
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Oil for deep frying

    Directions

    1. Score the fish 3–4 times on each side. Rub with garlic, lemon juice, spices, and salt. Marinate 15 minutes.
    2. Heat oil in a deep pan to 175°C.
    3. Carefully lower the fish into the oil. Fry for 8–10 minutes until cooked through and golden.
    4. Drain on paper towels. Serve with coconut rice, kachumbari, and lemon wedges.

    Coconut Rice (Wali wa Nazi)

    Fragrant Swahili coast rice cooked entirely in creamy coconut milk — simple yet luxurious.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: 25 min
    • Serves: 4
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups long-grain rice, washed
    • 400 ml coconut milk
    • 200 ml water
    • 1 tsp salt

    Directions

    1. Combine coconut milk, water, and salt in a pot. Bring to the boil.
    2. Add the washed rice, stir once, and reduce heat to low.
    3. Cover and simmer for 18–20 minutes until rice is cooked and liquid absorbed.
    4. Fluff with a fork. Serve as a side to fish, stew, or curry.

    Beef Stew

    A deeply flavourful Kenyan beef stew with chunky carrots, potatoes, and tomatoes.

    • ⏱ Prep: 15 min
    • Cook: 50 min
    • Serves: 5
    • Difficulty: Medium

    Ingredients

    • 500 g beef stew cuts
    • 2 large carrots, chopped
    • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
    • 2 tomatoes, diced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • Salt, pepper, and mixed spice to taste
    • 500 ml water or beef stock

    Directions

    1. Fry onions in oil until golden. Add beef and brown all sides for about 8 minutes.
    2. Add tomatoes and cook down into a sauce for 5 minutes. Season with spices.
    3. Add water or stock, bring to boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
    4. Add carrots and potatoes. Continue simmering for 20 more minutes until vegetables are tender and the stew has thickened.
    5. Adjust seasoning and serve with ugali or chapati.

    Fried Tilapia

    Whole tilapia simply seasoned with salt and lemon, then pan-fried to a crisp — a Lake Victoria classic.

    • ⏱ Prep: 10 min
    • Cook: 20 min
    • Serves: 2
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 2 whole tilapia, cleaned and scored
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 1 tsp salt
    • ½ cup cooking oil

    Directions

    1. Score the tilapia 3 times on each side. Rub with salt and lemon juice. Rest for 10 minutes.
    2. Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat.
    3. Fry the fish for 7–8 minutes on each side until golden and crispy.
    4. Serve immediately with ugali, sukuma wiki, and kachumbari.

    Arrowroots (Nduma)

    Humble boiled arrowroots — a treasured Kenyan breakfast staple enjoyed with tea across the country.

    • ⏱ Prep: 5 min
    • Cook: 30 min
    • Serves: 3
    • Difficulty: Easy

    Ingredients

    • 500 g arrowroots (nduma), peeled
    • Water to cover
    • 1 tsp salt

    Directions

    1. Peel arrowroots and cut into large chunks if very large.
    2. Place in a pot, cover with water, and add salt.
    3. Bring to the boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25–30 minutes until a fork pierces through easily.
    4. Drain and serve hot with Kenyan chai (black tea with milk and sugar).
